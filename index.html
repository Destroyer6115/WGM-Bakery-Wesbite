About U​s

The Bakery Living Classroom was officially Established in January of 2022 and served as an opportunity for junior crew (youth from across Hardy County, WV between the ages of 14-and 18) employed by the Wardensville Garden Market to have the chance to experience and learn about the baking and culinary fields. 

In the beginning, there were 6. Three existing junior crew, and three brand new came together under the Bakery Liaison to learn, experiment, and produce products, not only for their own education and skill building, but also to sell in the Wardensville Garden Market. The bakery living classroom covers a broad range of culinary techniques and recipes, from the granola, dog treats, and hand-decorated sugar cookies seen in the market, to a variety of breads, ​pastries, cupcakes, pies and more. 
The Bakery Living Classroom also allows the junior crew within, to work alongside the staff during the myriad of events held by the Wardensville Garden Market and Farms Work Wonders. The bakery junior crew's hard work and passion has directly led to the overwhelming success of Earth Day, Art at the Garden Market, 4th of July, and the Down on the Farm dinner all hosted in 2022.

In the first 10 months, the products that were produced by the bakery junior crew for the bakery team and the market led to a potential revenue of over $8,000, all of which goes back into the Farms Work Wonders organization and the scholarships that it offers. 
The Bakery Living classroom continues to expand and change as junior crew graduate highschool and begin their paths beyond, and new junior crew take up the helm. There is always so much to see through the big windows of our beautiful bakery on Main St. in Wardensville, WV. Just Take a look!

Contact Bakery Liaison

Joshua Herrell

A native of western Maryland, Joshua started his education at Frostburg State University, achieving a Bachelor’s of Fine Arts with a Minor in art history. After meeting his now spouse Dustin, Joshua decided on a career change and enrolled in the culinary/baking program at Allegany College of Maryland in Cumberland, MD. While continuing his education, Joshua began working at various resorts, finally landing at Omni Bedford Springs Resort in Bedford, PA. Joshua rose through the ranks and became the Executive Pastry Chef, overseeing a team and production for various outlets within the resort.

Joshua and his partner adopted a little boy in May of 2021 named Rowan fulfilling his dream of becoming a dad. After working through the Covid pandemic, he left the resort in July of 2022, moved, and joined the Farms Work Wonders team in Wardensville, WV in September, 2022 as the Bakery Liaison for the Farms Work Wonders Mission department. 
Joshua enjoys photography as well as hiking, kayaking, cooking, and exploring the natural beauty of Appalaccia.
